CyberKnife Surgery-Understanding the procedure

In August 2001, the FDA gave clearance to Cyberknife to deal with tumors in any kind of part of the body. The Cyberknife Robotic system is made up of primarily 2 components, the robot arm and also the radiation generated by the particle accelerator. Although the name of this system consists of the word 'surgical treatment', there is no cutting involved in this system of therapy.

Prior to the therapy begins, the individual needs to go through a high-resolution CT scan to make sure that the physicians can ascertain the particular details of the lump, such as its place, shapes and size. This data is after that moved to the workstation of the Cyberknife system. This information is utilized to devise a therapy prepare for the patient. The doctor will certainly determine the needed radiation dosage for the tumor which will certainly not damage the healthy cells around the lump.

For the treatment to be performed, the individual is made to sit conveniently while the robot arm of the Cyberknife walks around the person to the numerous places from which it will direct the radiation to the location of the growth. Relying on the lump, this whole procedure may take in between 30 and also 90 mins. Often, the therapy might be topped numerous days. In this instance, the person may have to return for numerous sessions which are topped lots of days.
