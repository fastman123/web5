The International Health, Racket and Sports Club Association (IHRSA) held an exhibition in Nashville in 1986. It was throughout this convention that StairMaster revealed its new production, and released the StairMaster 4000PT. This stair stepper was constructed without an escalator like, revolving staircase. Instead of a mechanical speed managing the speed of a rotating staircase as was the case with the SM 5000 and SM 6000, the SM 4000PT had 2 actions, with motions independent of one another, mimicing climbing stairs. Mechanically speaking, its aggregate pedal movement was controlled through a shared rotating shaft, while speed controls were configured through its alternator. StairMaster would have a good deal of success with their brand-new 4000PT stair climber and the 2 independent actions design, would set the trend for the next years. As interest in the turning staircase designs soon started to fade, sales for the StairMaster 4000PT escalated.
