What exactly is an eyelid surgery or blepharoplasty?
Eyelid surgery refers to blepharoplasty which is surgery in the eye area to remove overhanging and excess skin and fat deposits below and above the eye. This can include a loss of volume beneath thee eye in the tear trough region. Eyelid surgery may not always completely solve the volume issue and eye filler may also be used in some cases. Blepharoplasty is the 2nd most common cosmetic plastic surgery procedure done in the US each year.

What is the main reason of having eyelid surgery?

In order to give the eyes a more refreshed look, the hanging skin above the eye can be removed to address the tired look and to improve vision for an upper eyelid surgery procedure. For a lower blepharoplasty, the fat pads on the lower eyelids is taken out as well as any loose skin. This will improve any sort of the puffiness and building lower eyelid that is common with the aging process.

Do my fatty eyebags get removed?

Yes, fat deposits in the lower eyelids will be removed, this is often what is referred to when eyebags are discussed and building eyelids. This is performed during a lower blepharoplasty. Upper blepharoplasty can be performed at the same time in order to remove excess hanging skin above the eye.

Why get eyelid surgery before getting a facelift?
Generally speaking, the eyes may show the signs of aging sooner. This occurs much before the skin along the face becomes saggy and jowls form. The eyelid surgery may happen many years before the need for a facelift.
