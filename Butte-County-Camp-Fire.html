A person who suffers a loss or has actually been injured in some way due to the negligence or actions of another has recourse under the law in the U.S. If he can show that monetary or physical loss he has suffered was the fault of another person or company, he can approach an accident attorney to look for countervailing and compensatory damages on his behalf. For example, if he remains in a vehicle accident caused by a drunk driver and is injured or disabled at the same time, he can look for compensation from the other driver.

Accident law, also known as tort law, covers claims occurring from a vast array of circumstances such as wrongful death, medical malpractice, and faulty items to mishaps, discrimination, and worker's settlement. A victim can make a claim for loss of income arising from physical disability or psychological distress or for damage to residential or commercial property.


An experienced injury lawyer will settle a claim out of court, as most accuseds wish to avoid promotion and costly trials.
