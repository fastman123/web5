What Is Alpha Gpc

Alpha-GPC (Alpha-glycerophosphocholine or often just glycerophosphocholine) is a choline- including supplement that, although discovered in a range of food that are likewise abundant in choline, seems pharmacologically active at greater dosages. Alpha-GPC is mostly marketed due to it giving dietary choline to the body following oral consumption, and being the most efficient choline prodrug at doing so (able to influence both systemic and brain concentrations of choline).

Oral supplements of alpha-GPC is of interest for nootropic purposes, as it appears to have cognitive-enhancing homes (no human evidence exists to support this in otherwise-healthy youth, however it does have support in rodents) and attenuate the rate of cognitive decline in the elderly. In regards to lowering the rate of cognitive decline, alpha-GPC at high doses (1,200 mg) does seem somewhat efficient in moderate to moderate Alzheimer's disease and works nicely with the standard therapy (acetylcholinesterase inhibitors).

The loss of nerve cells that use the neurotransmitter acetylcholine is an early occasion in the advancement of Alzheimer's disease . However, no studies have actually examined whether alpha-GPC improves cognition in healthy individuals or avoids Alzheimer's disease. In one open-label study, clients with mild cognitive impairment reported that alpha-GPC treatment a little enhanced cognition over 90 days, and this result appeared to be more pronounced in patients without APOE4, the hereditary threat element for Alzheimer's disease .See more at Alpha-GPC

Alpha-GPC More Info

Preclinical studies suggest that alpha-GPC increases the levels of acetylcholine in the brain and can enhance cognition in models of memory problems or brain aging [3; 4]. In a design of accelerated aging, alpha-GPC had no effect on brain levels of beta-amyloid, a biological marker of Alzheimer's illness . However, in a model of high blood pressure, alpha-GPC lowered inflammation around capillary in the brain, although it did not preserve the integrity of brain blood vessels.

One study reported that alpha-GPC improved cognition and function in clients with mild to moderate dementia over six months. Nevertheless, the clients were not taking presently authorized Alzheimer's drugs which also increase levels of acetylcholine in the brain .
