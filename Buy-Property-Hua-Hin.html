Finding Hua Hin Property For sale
A couple of hours away from Bangkok, Hua Hin is a beautiful self-sustaining city, that offers foreigners one of the best investment options. Not only has the supply for properties like condos increased over the years, but there are many places with lucrative pieces of property.

Even though it is considered a relatively small town, it a fast expanding urban sprawl covering roughly 8 kilometers around the designated city center. Plus, there are new development projects breaking ground each month, many of which are further away from the city center. Expats and locals alike may be interested in upcoming areas like Cha-am, Khao Tao, Khao Takiab, Khao Kalok and Pranburi. However, you need to find a place that suits your lifestyle the best and in addition, has the following:

-The town center should be nearby along with nightlife and shopping facilities
-Easy access to public transport
-If you have kids, then schools should be nearby
-Low noise and a safe street
-Amenities like water, telephone, internet, and electricity

Searching for Houses for Sale Hua Hin
The traditional and albeit the easy way to find property for sale in Hua Hin is to contact a property agent or real estate agent as they are called. A couple of agents should be able to pull up options based on your needs or budget.
Alternatively, you can search online. A search for “property for sale in Hua Hin” should yield a number of classified websites. However, many of the properties in those classifieds are posted by real-estate agents whom you’ll need to contact, and then they can show you around the property.
Fees and Deposits


Once you have decided on buying a piece of property, beware that there are expenses incurred when selling or buying property. The transfer and sale of the property are subject to the following fees:
-0.5% in the way of stamp duty.
-3.3% specific business tax on the total asset value or sale price whichever is higher.
-Income tax is often calculated using a complex formula that you can estimate to be around 2% for low priced properties and about 3% for higher-priced properties.
-2% transfer fees.

Conclusion
The key to finding houses for sale Hua Hin is to first check out as many properties as you can within your budget. Once you find something that looks good, negotiate a price, and make sure that you have a legal advisor to help you vet the paperwork. That will ensure that everything is done by the book, and your investment is secure.
