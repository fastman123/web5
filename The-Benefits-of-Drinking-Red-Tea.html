Rooibos tea is not truly a tea at all, however rather a natural tisane from the plant Aspalathus linearis. Aspalathus linearis is a small bush that grows only in South Africa. While the name Rooibos means "red bush", the leaves are green when selected and only end up being red when fermented. The resulting tea is remarkably red in color.

South Africans have actually been taking in Red tea for medical and refreshment functions for a long time, but it started to be cultivated particularly for tea after being discovered in 1904 by Benjamin Ginsberg. Ginsberg was a Russian immigrant who so enjoyed the tea that he started to cultivate and market it.
