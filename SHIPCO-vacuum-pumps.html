HEATING AND COOLING air pump will certainly avoid freeze-up

Freeze-up inside your a/c system could happen due to water vapor existing in the system. As the cooling agent goes through the regular process of compression, home heating, evaporation and cooling, the water vapor could start taking shape at the coldest component around the growth unit and the icy crystals cause a "freeze-up". In the best situation, this can stop the system from operating. In the worst instance, this can harm the compressor or the evaporator device. In any case, to stop freeze-ups, all the moisture must be gotten rid of from the system prior to recharging it with a new cooling agent. This will be finished with a top quality, high vacuum electrical pump.

Prevent corrosion

Rust, besides the freeze-up is another repercussion of excessive water vapor residue inside the cooling system. The water itself will certainly have corroding effect on the within metal walls of the air conditioning system. Worse, when combined with other extremely reactive compounds inside such as chlorine substances and also fluorine compounds, water vapor can cause irreparable corrosion damages. Which type of damages, because it is hidden from the eye, won't disclose itself up until it is too late. A regularly utilized high vacuum pump will avoid rust by getting rid of the water vapor from the system.

Protect against mixing of old as well as brand-new refrigerant

When upgrading the refrigerant to a new sort of refrigerant, or when replacing the old cooling agent with a fresh cooling agent, making use of a HVAC pump will certainly permit full elimination of the old cooling agent. That will enable the system to run to the specs as they apply to the pure brand-new refrigerant. The air conditioning system will certainly be able to run more effectively this way.
