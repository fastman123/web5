Mission Trips

Planning an effective short-term or summer mission journey can be a difficult task, specifically if that isn't the degree of your task description. Some discover it so daunting that they choose to scrap the concept of short-term objectives entirely. Why not leave objectives to those committed to long term objectives? Aren't they more efficient? Sadly, that "leave-ministry-to-the-paid-experts" attitude has left the American church with a lot of pew-sitters and too couple of workers. Reduction in resources going to short term objectives doesn't necessarily suggest more resources are spent on long term - or profession - objectives. Fortunately, there is help for preparing short term and summer season missions.

Here are some things that can help:

Prayer. Let's start with the apparent primary step. "If anyone lacks wisdom, let him ask of God," is from James 1:5. However prayer is far more than a demand to the Grandpa-in-the-Sky. It is looking for to know and align ourselves with God's will. So what does he have prepared for individuals you shepherd? What are the challenges he sees in their lives? What does he prepare to do about it? God has a strategy and you can ask him to tell it to you.

But ministry isn't practically where however also when. God's timing is always best, even if it does not make good sense to us. Missionaries from William Carey to Jim Eliot can vouch for how insane God's timing appears in some cases. So before you begin planning, the first question to ask is should your team go on a summertime objective or not? That is a fair question, and one you and your team need to ask.

Specialists. Short-term missions has changed so rapidly in the last twenty years that I don't know anybody who can declare to be an expert in it. But there are lots of people with a great deal of practical details. A long term missionary in the location you are going can provide indispensable recommendations on what to expect, what to wear, and how to act to reduce cross-cultural barriers. And do not overlook your siblings and sisters who are native to the country. Excellent interaction with the residents is invaluable in any mission either long or short term. There are also professionals in travel and travel insurance that you can speak with for those needs. Agencies that specialize in short-term objectives integrate a few of these competence. Your task is to make sure that you get what you require.

Long Term Relationships. The spiritual health of those going and of those getting short term missions depends on establishing long term relationships. The days of month-long journeys on ships to spread the gospel are long gone. Today there are few places on the earth that can't be reached in 24 hours, if you have the stamina for the journey. The internet and e-mail have actually even more lowered the true distance between buddies. Now the obstacle is are we ready to cooperate with and learn from each other? Your church may not reach numerous nations, but are you willing to get included with one?

Discipleship. Imagine yourself watching out over your parish and asking, "The number of of these have been discipled? How many understand what they think and could share it with another person?" In Matthew 28:19, Jesus commands us to make disciples as part of his Great Commission. Short-term objectives can be part of the procedure both for those going and for those getting, however it needs to be intentional. Individuals can't be disciple by osmosis; otherwise Jesus would not have given it as a command.

Training. In addition to discipleship, other kinds of training might be needed. Everything from how to comprehend a various culture to abilities like drama or building may be required. Even the proper method to clear brush or trash so as not to be hurt need to be taught. Getting along with team members is so vital to a successful trip, that unique attention needs to be given to help members discover how to fix conflicts. The specialists may remain in your congregation. Find out who they are and get them to offer their time. Your job is to ensure all who go on the journey understand their job, how to do it and how to do it with others.

Humbleness. Humbleness is a misunderstood and ignored virtue, yet without it the world would break down. Humbleness on a summertime objective means that we seek to minister with - and not to - our bros and siblings in Christ. Humbleness means that we pursue peace with team members, rapidly resolving conflicts and doing those things that result in harmony and unity. Humbleness means we seek to discover as much as we seek to teach.
