Cork is the external bark of an evergreen oak recognized by the Latin name Quercus (oak) Suber (cork). The first stripping occurs when the tree is in between 15 as well as 20 years of age. Subsequent yields comply with at 8 to ten years intervals. Today, the center of the world's cork oak forest is focused in Southern Europe; Portugal, Spain, Italy & France, which makes up 65% of the cork oak production. North Africa has the remaining 35%. The total land surface area inhabited by this oak is 2.2 million hectares (5.434 million acres!) of which Portugal and Spain alone stand for 54%.
